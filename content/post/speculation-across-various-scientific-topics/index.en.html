Given all of the data that E&L [Evans & Levinson, 2009] cite, how could anyone maintain the notion of a universal grammar with linguistic content? Traditionally, there have been three basic strategies. First, just as we may force English grammar into the Procrustean bed of Latin grammar – that is how I was taught the structure of English in grade school – the grammars of the world’s so-called exotic languages may be forced into an abstract scheme based mainly on European languages.

— Tomasello (2009)

reference_query = '"language evolution" OR "evolution of language"'
